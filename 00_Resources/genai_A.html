Generative AI: In the classroom and beyond#

Productivity and Creativity#

Pros
  • Automates routine repetive tasks
  • Access to Data
  • Cost reduction. Humans have to do less work.
  • Idea generation, use as a collaborative partner.
  • On-ramp skills for non-experts.
  • Diverse specialties across different AI platforms.
Cons
  • Accuracy. ChatGPT is a people-pleaser.
  • Human atrophy (dumbing down)
  • Plagiarism.
  • Disrupts workflow. Integration challenge.
  • Overuse. Shoehorning. Funneling money into a 'fad'?
  • Homogenization of outputs.
  • generic, often low-quality answers.

Learning and Employment#

Pros
  • Customizable tutor, 24/7.
  • Layout plans for projects, gets you over first hurdle.
  • Makes people more productive.
  • Gathering/summarizing information.
  • Levels the playing field.
  • Small bug fixes.
  • Literacy, language barriers.
Cons
  • Easy to plagiarize.
  • Easy to get lazy.
  • Not always familiar with context.
  • Lack of security.
  • Job displacement...both in terms of skill displacement, applicants broadly spamming openings, AI resume filters.

External byproducts#

Pros
  • Overall cheaper costs.
  • Optimization of processes.
  • Reducing inequality. (building resumes, providing access)
  • Saves your data...tailors experience.
  • Ease of use, levels the playing field.
Cons
  • Saves your data, what are they using it for?
  • Up to the user how they use, few guardrails.
  • Environmental uses power and water.
  • Scams, deep fakes, misinformation etc.
  • Lose the opportunity to learn skills.
  • Cheating and fake expertise.
  • Takes work from other people with little credit.

Accessibility and Equity#

Pros
  • It supports all students, personalized platforms
  • Bridges language barriers
  • Accessible to all ages
  • Accessible to differently abled
  • Can replace costly services
Cons
  • Some don't have access to digital devices/internet (rural, low-income)
  • Few guard rails
  • Tiered service not equal
  • Bias in data and consequent discrimination
  • Global inequity. The audience isn't everyone.

Follow-up questions: In the classroom#

  • What are productive uses of GenAI?

  • What are counter-productive uses of GenAI?

    • Not using is counter-productive for employment. You’ll be expected to know it.

  • How does student usage of GenAI affect other students?

    • Is it fair for those who choose not to use it and produce ‘worse’ work.

    • Incentivizing grades vs learning.

  • How does student usage of GenAI affect instructors?

  • How does instructor usage of GenAI affect students?

  • What are your responsibilities with using AI? What is proper usage? What should be the penalty for improper usage?

    • At the end of the day, people will use it. Use it to learn and do better than others. Penalty for yourself.

  • Have you discussed GenAI policy in other classes? What are the policies you’ve seen?

    • Allowed, but according Honor Code. Describe your usage.

    • Don’t use to create, use to enhance.

    • All use strictly forbidden, consequence was Honor Coded.

    • Encouraged AI to use for study.

    • Assignment required genAI and criticize.

    • Use to assemble portfolio.

    • Discouraged in intro courses. Later on, use it but you must understand it.
